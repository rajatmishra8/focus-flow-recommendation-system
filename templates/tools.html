Focus Tips

Phone-Free Zones

Put your phone in another room while studying. Out of sight means out of mind — research shows this boosts IQ-level task performance.

Background Sounds

Instrumental music, brown noise, or rain sounds reduce mental fatigue. Avoid lyrics — they compete with verbal processing.

Stay Hydrated

Even mild dehydration (1-2%) impairs concentration and short-term memory. Keep a water bottle on your desk.

Your Study Space

A clean, well-lit, and organized workspace reduces cognitive load by up to 30%. Natural light is best.

Write It Down

Before starting, write your top 3 goals for the session. This primes your brain and reduces task-switching.

Move Between Sessions

A 5-min walk during breaks boosts creativity and alertness. Don't just scroll — move your body.

Study Planning Guide

1

Weekly Brain Dump

Every Sunday, write down all tasks, deadlines, and goals. Getting them out of your head frees cognitive space.

2

Time Block Your Calendar

Assign specific subjects to specific time slots. Treat study blocks like appointments you can't miss.

3

Apply the 2-Minute Rule

If a task takes less than 2 minutes, do it immediately. This prevents the mental weight of a growing to-do list.

4

Daily Review (5 mins)

Before bed, check what you accomplished and adjust tomorrow's plan. Consistency over perfection.

5

Use Spaced Repetition

Review notes at increasing intervals (1 day, 3 days, 1 week). This dramatically improves long-term retention.
